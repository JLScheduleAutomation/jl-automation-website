Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly do you deliver schedules?

We deliver all schedules via email at least 48 hours before the start of each work week, giving you ample time for review and any necessary adjustments.

What if I need changes to a schedule?

We include one round of revisions with each schedule. Simply email us your requested changes within 24 hours of delivery, and we'll provide an updated schedule.

How do you handle last-minute call-outs or emergencies?

While our primary service is weekly planning, we offer emergency scheduling support for existing clients. Contact us immediately for urgent scheduling needs.

What information do you need from me?

We need employee details, availability preferences, business hours, staffing requirements per shift, and any specific scheduling rules or labor regulations that apply to your business.

Do you work with businesses of all sizes?

Absolutely! We serve businesses with 5 to 100+ employees. Our pricing is tiered based on team size and scheduling complexity.

How do you ensure compliance with labor laws?

We stay current with federal and state labor regulations, including break requirements, overtime rules, and maximum hour limits. We'll also incorporate any specific regulations for your industry.

Can you handle seasonal scheduling fluctuations?

Yes! We specialize in seasonal businesses and can adjust staffing levels based on your busy and slow periods, ensuring optimal coverage year-round.

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept payment through Venmo for simple and secure transactions. Payment is due upon schedule delivery each week. Our Venmo handle will be provided once your service is confirmed.

Information Needed from Clients

To create the perfect schedule for your business, we need the following information:

  1. Employee Details: Complete list of all staff members with their roles and positions
  2. Availability: Each employee's preferred working hours, days off, and any restrictions
  3. Business Hours: Your operating hours and required shift coverage
  4. Staffing Requirements: Minimum number of employees needed per shift/role
  5. Rotation Rules: Any specific rotation patterns or scheduling preferences
  6. Labor Regulations: Local labor laws, break requirements, and overtime rules
  7. Special Constraints: Any unique requirements or scheduling challenges
